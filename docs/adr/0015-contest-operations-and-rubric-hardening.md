# Contest operations policy + rubric hardening: three-class amendments, submit-when-done rounds, pre-tag sealed custody, locus-adherence grading, objective anchors in every scored dimension

**Context.** ADRs 0012–0014 defined *what* the contest builds but left operations undecided (defect handling after forks exist, round close, sealed-set custody, provisioning), and the §7 rubric had two weaknesses for its actual purpose — exposing quality differences between frontier models: coverage-as-breadth stops discriminating under a fixed issue set, and the two dimensions most about code quality (panel-scored quality and tests) were the most exposed to LLM-judge noise (verbosity/self-preference bias).

**Decision.** **Operations:** (1) amendments are three-class — *clarification* (public broadcast, no artifact change), *gold correction* (fix + re-grade everyone at round close), *substrate change* (new tagged dataset version, bounded remediation window, remediation effort reported separately) — all logged publicly and simultaneously; (2) rounds close **submit-when-done** with operator discretion as the backstop (no formal effort caps; effort/tokens are logged per harness, rough where necessary, priced via one table into notional cost — reported, never scored); the sealed set releases to all contestants simultaneously at close; (3) the sealed change-request set is authored **before the fork tag**, held outside the repo, with the archive's sha256 committed publicly pre-tag and release-by-commit at round close; (4) round-1 provisioning is **one Databricks workspace, per-contestant catalog + service principal** with scoped UC grants; environments must be fully rebuildable from the fork; credentials never enter the repo; **forks stay private mid-round**, published at close. **Rubric:** dimension 3 (coverage) merges into dimension 2 as *spec fidelity & completeness*, anchored by the contest issues' acceptance-criteria checklist (breadth becomes a reported fact); change absorption is scored as post-change gold correctness **plus locus adherence** — each sealed change request declares the seam it should land at, out-of-locus touches are enumerated and reviewed (raw line counts reported only); the panel scores by **pairwise comparison with per-judge scores published**; test quality gains a **seeded-bug/perturbation probe**; runnability gains a **fresh-agent reproduction probe**. The optional RDF/OWL track is **deferred out of v1** (moves from open questions to out-of-scope).

**Why.** Every scored dimension now has an objective anchor with the panel as tiebreaker (the ADR 0013 principle applied to the rubric itself); locus adherence turns diff size — architecture-confounded as a raw number — into a behavioral test of the semantic-seam thesis; pre-tag hashing makes "the change set wasn't tailored to observed outputs" verifiable rather than asserted; and submit-when-done fits a self-orchestrated contest where the operator is the scheduler and effort is a comparison signal, not a constraint.
